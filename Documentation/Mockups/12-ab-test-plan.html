Test schedule

48-hour window, calibrated to your channel velocity (median 12.4K views in first 48h).

Hour 0

Publish

Hour 12

First read

Glance at impressions. Don't act yet — too early for signal.

Hour 24

Majority decision

If one variant leads CTR by ≥10%, lock it in. Otherwise wait.

Hour 48

Final

Sample size sufficient. Promote winner, log learning.

Decision rules

  • promote

    Promote variant A if its CTR beats the others by ≥10% with at least 2,500 impressions per variant.

  • hold

    If variants are within 3% of each other, ship the result variant (default) and log inconclusive.

  • regenerate

    If all three variants underperform channel baseline by ≥15%, regenerate titles and thumbnails — the idea framing may be off.

Sample-size note

At your channel velocity, expect ~7,500 impressions per variant by hour 48 — enough for a 10%+ delta to be meaningful, but not enough to detect <5% deltas confidently.

Cross-test learning — what this test will teach you

Your last 5 outliers all used result-framing. This test deliberately includes one stretch (curiosity) and one off-voice (fear) variant so you learn whether your audience's preference is real or just a habit on your part. Even if variant 3 wins by a wide margin, you'll have evidence to stop questioning the pattern. If variant 1 (curiosity) wins, you've found a lever you weren't using.
